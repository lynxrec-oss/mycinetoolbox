# Blog Wireframe Layout Guidelines

This skill guides the design, layout, and placement of images within blog articles to maintain a professional, high-fidelity visual structure.

## 🎨 Image Categories & Styling
- **Product Beauty Shots (Cyan / `#4fa3c4`):** Front-facing, angled, and detail macro shots of gear. Used to illustrate build quality and mechanical components.
- **Sample Stills (Orange / `#c9622f`):** Graded frame grabs from actual video footage. Used to showcase and prove performance claims (sharpness, flare control, chromatic aberration).
- **Group/Lineup Shots (Green / `#7d8f4d`):** Banners showing multiple units or size/range comparisons side-by-side.

## 📐 Placement Principles
1. **Hero Image (Lineup / Group Shot):** Sits right under the title before the byline to introduce the subject (Suggested: 16:9 banner).
2. **Product Beauty Shot:** Placed right after the introduction section before the text gets technical (Suggested: Square or 3:2 centered).
3. **Group Lineup Banner:** Placed near lineup discussions to visually prove features like unified size and weight (Suggested: Wide banner).
4. **Detail / Macro Shots:** Placed near mechanical discussions like focus markings or filter slots (Suggested: 2 images side-by-side).
5. **Sample Footage Stills:** Placed immediately following performance claims (e.g., showing center sharpness details or flares) as evidence.
